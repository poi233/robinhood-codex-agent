# Earnings Quality and Red Flags

## Earnings Quality

High-quality earnings are repeatable, cash-linked, and not overly dependent on accounting presentation.

## Red Flags

- revenue growth paired with weak cash conversion
- margin improvement driven by temporary cuts instead of economic improvement
- recurring "one-time" adjustments
- aggressive stock-based compensation dilution
- acquisition-driven EPS optics without real per-share value creation
- receivables, inventory, or capitalized items growing faster than the business

## Management Claim Filter

Management narratives should be sorted into:

- clearly evidenced
- plausible but unproven
- inconsistent with the statements

## Common Error

Using adjusted earnings as if they were equal to owner earnings.
