# Unit Economics and Growth Quality

## Growth Is Not Automatically Quality

Ask what each additional unit of growth costs and what it earns.

## Good Growth

Good growth usually has:

- stable or improving gross margin
- reasonable sales efficiency
- attractive incremental returns
- strong retention or repeat usage
- cash generation that eventually follows scale

## Weak Growth

Weak growth often has:

- rising revenue but collapsing margins
- customer acquisition that gets more expensive
- dependence on subsidies, promotions, or financing
- low conversion from revenue to cash

## Reinvestment Logic

The key question is not "can it grow?" but "what does the company earn on the capital required to grow?"

## Practical Read

When reviewing a company, write:

- source of growth
- durability of growth
- cost of growth
- cash value of growth
