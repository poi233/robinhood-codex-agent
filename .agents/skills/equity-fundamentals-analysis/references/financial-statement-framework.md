# Financial Statement Framework

## Read All Three Statements Together

The income statement shows performance claims.
The balance sheet shows accumulated decisions and obligations.
The cash flow statement shows how much of reported success converts into usable cash.

## Income Statement

Key questions:

- is revenue growth organic, acquired, price-driven, or volume-driven?
- are gross margins stable, improving, or masking pressure?
- does operating leverage appear real or temporary?

## Balance Sheet

Key questions:

- what assets really matter?
- how much leverage exists?
- are receivables, inventory, or intangibles growing in ways that need explanation?

## Cash Flow Statement

Key questions:

- does net income turn into operating cash flow?
- what capex is required to maintain the business?
- is free cash flow genuine or flattered by working-capital timing?

## Cross-Checks

- revenue up, receivables up faster: possible collection or demand-quality issue
- earnings up, cash weak: possible low-quality earnings
- margins up, capex or R&D down: ask if the business is underinvesting

## Common Error

Reading one headline metric in isolation and calling it fundamental analysis.
