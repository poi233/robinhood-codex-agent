# Analysis Workflow

## Recommended Order

1. Understand the business model.
2. Identify revenue and margin drivers.
3. Read the three statements together.
4. Judge unit economics and growth quality.
5. Review capital allocation.
6. Check balance-sheet risk.
7. Choose valuation framework.
8. Write the risked conclusion.

## Why This Order Works

It prevents two common mistakes:

- starting from stock price and reverse-rationalizing the business
- starting from valuation multiples before understanding the economics

## Output Structure

A basic investor memo should answer:

- what the company really is
- why the economics are good or bad
- whether growth is valuable
- whether management allocates well
- what valuation method fits
- what could break the thesis
