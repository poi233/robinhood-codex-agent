# Capital Allocation

## Why It Matters

Capital allocation determines whether value created by the business actually accrues to shareholders.

## What to Review

- buybacks
- issuance and dilution
- dividends
- debt-funded activity
- acquisitions
- internal reinvestment

## Good Allocation

- buying back stock at sensible prices
- disciplined M&A with strategic logic and evidence
- using debt without threatening resilience
- funding projects with attractive returns

## Bad Allocation

- buying back stock only after overvaluation
- serial dilution while claiming shareholder alignment
- empire-building acquisitions
- debt taken on to maintain appearances

## Useful Question

If management generated one extra dollar of free cash flow, where would it go, and would that likely create value?
