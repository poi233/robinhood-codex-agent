# Capital Allocation Cases

## Case 1: AAPL, shareholder-focused allocation

Pattern tags:
- `capital-allocation`
- `high-quality-compounder`

Context:
- AAPL continues to use buybacks as a major mechanism for returning value

Why it matters:
- buybacks make sense when paired with enormous free cash generation and durable economics
- the company has a long history of turning operating strength into per-share support

Operational takeaway:
- capital allocation quality can be a major reason a mature company stays investable

Why this case is strong:
- massive operating cash flow plus consistent repurchases create a clear per-share value channel
- debt exists, but the scale and reliability of cash generation keep the structure understandable

Investor lesson:
- mature growth stories often become long-duration compounders because management keeps converting cash strength into per-share support

## Case 2: AVGO, acquisition plus scale

Pattern tags:
- `capital-allocation`
- `balance-sheet-risk`

Context:
- Broadcom has used acquisitions to build scale and breadth
- debt and integration complexity matter alongside the operating story

Why it matters:
- this is not a simple organic-only compounder
- management judgment must be read through deal discipline, integration, and debt tolerance

Operational takeaway:
- acquisitive capital allocation can create enormous value, but only if the balance sheet and integration logic stay coherent

What to watch:
- whether acquired earnings convert cleanly into free cash flow
- whether leverage trends down through time or becomes a permanent feature
- whether management continues to buy quality at rational prices instead of defending empire size

## Case 3: TSLA, capital allocation judged by execution optionality

Pattern tags:
- `capital-allocation`
- `narrative-vs-economics`

Context:
- in high-multiple businesses, every capital decision is judged against ambitious future expectations

Operational takeaway:
- the higher the narrative premium, the harsher the market becomes about allocation discipline and execution quality

What makes TSLA different:
- investors often underweight capital allocation when they are focused on product optionality
- but in a high-multiple stock, every deployment choice is implicitly competing against a demanding valuation

## Case 4: NVDA, restrained balance-sheet use

Pattern tags:
- `capital-allocation`
- `high-quality-compounder`

Context:
- NVDA currently combines massive profitability with comparatively light debt

Why it matters:
- not every high-growth leader needs aggressive balance-sheet engineering to scale

Operational takeaway:
- clean capital structure is itself a capital-allocation signal; sometimes the best decision is not to get clever
