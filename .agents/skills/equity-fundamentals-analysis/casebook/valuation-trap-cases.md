# Valuation Trap Cases

## Case 1: TSLA, June 2026 snapshot

Pattern tags:
- `valuation-trap`
- `narrative-vs-economics`

Context:
- price around `406`
- forward PE around `163x`
- trailing PE around `369x`
- return on equity far below the quality leaders in this research universe

Why it is a trap candidate:
- the stock can still be attractive to narratives, but the valuation leaves little room for execution disappointment
- economics are not poor, but the market asks for a lot

Operational takeaway:
- valuation traps are not always low-multiple stocks; they can also be elite-story stocks priced on heroic assumptions

What makes the trap persistent:
- forward PE around `162.6x` and trailing PE around `369.5x`
- operating margin only about `4.2%`
- profit margin only about `3.9%`
- revenue growth around `15.8%`, which is not enough by itself to justify infinite narrative slack

Correct reading:
- the stock can still rally violently, but the fundamental margin of safety is thin unless profitability re-accelerates

## Case 2: AVGO, early June 2026 post-earnings

Pattern tags:
- `valuation-trap`
- `great-business-bad-entry`

Context:
- AVGO delivered strong AI and revenue numbers
- the stock still fell sharply after the run into `495`

Lesson:
- a strong business does not protect a buyer who paid for perfection right before the report

Operational takeaway:
- one of the cleanest valuation-trap forms is "excellent company, wrong timing, impossible expectations"

Deeper lesson:
- the trap was not low business quality
- the trap was entering after the stock had already priced a nearly flawless earnings and guidance outcome

Research discipline:
- before calling a stock attractive after a strong run, ask what kind of quarter the market already requires just to keep the price flat

## Case 3: NVDA as a non-trap until expectations outrun evidence

Pattern tags:
- `valuation-trap`
- `growth-quality`

Context:
- NVDA trades at premium valuation metrics on sales and market cap, but current earnings power is also unusually high

Why it is not the same trap as TSLA:
- margins and cash generation are already elite
- balance-sheet stress is low
- the premium is attached to proven economics, not only aspiration

What would turn it into a trap:
- if demand durability weakens while investors still price the stock for uninterrupted infrastructure acceleration

Operational takeaway:
- a premium multiple is not automatically a trap; a trap begins when price assumes economics that future evidence cannot support
