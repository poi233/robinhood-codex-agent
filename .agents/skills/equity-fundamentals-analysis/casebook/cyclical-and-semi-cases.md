# Cyclical And Semi Cases

## Case 1: AVGO, June 2026 snapshot

Pattern tags:
- `cyclical-quality`
- `semi-cycle`

Context:
- AVGO remains fundamentally strong with AI exposure and scale, but the stock violently repriced after earnings

Why it is useful:
- Broadcom is not a low-quality business
- but the stock still behaves like a high-expectation semi with customer concentration and integration questions

Lesson:
- great semi businesses can still re-rate hard when expectations are priced too tightly

Operational takeaway:
- "quality cyclicals" deserve different treatment from fragile cyclicals, but they are still cyclicals in market behavior

Economics snapshot:
- forward PE about `19.7x`
- gross margin about `76.3%`
- operating margin about `49.0%`
- revenue growth about `47.9%`
- free cash flow about `27.2B`

Why this case matters:
- these are outstanding economics, but the stock still suffered a violent post-earnings reset
- this teaches that cyclical exposure plus customer concentration plus expectation saturation can dominate even a brilliant print

Risk frame:
- integration and leverage still matter because total debt near `64.9B` is not trivial
- if the market questions durability of the AI revenue ramp or customer concentration, the valuation multiple can compress faster than the business deteriorates

## Case 2: NVDA, June 2026 snapshot

Pattern tags:
- `semi-cycle`
- `growth-quality`

Context:
- NVDA has unusually strong economics for a semiconductor name

Lesson:
- some semis deserve compounder-like treatment, but they still live in a space where demand timing and capacity assumptions matter

Operational takeaway:
- do not flatten all semis into one risk bucket; distinguish structural winners from ordinary cycle participants

Comparison with AVGO:
- NVDA currently looks more balance-sheet-light and expectation-heavy around pure compute leadership
- AVGO looks more diversified but more integration- and customer-mix-sensitive

Operational takeaway extension:
- sector labels matter less than economic structure; two semiconductor leaders can deserve very different investor treatment

## Case 3: Semi quality can still create stock volatility

Pattern tags:
- `semi-cycle`
- `valuation-trap`

Context:
- both NVDA and AVGO can post excellent economics and still experience sharp stock reactions

Lesson:
- semis teach one of the best fundamental investing rules: a strong business does not immunize the stock from expectation resets

Operational takeaway:
- when analyzing semis, always separate business quality from setup quality
