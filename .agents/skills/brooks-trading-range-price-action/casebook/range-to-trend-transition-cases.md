# Range to Trend Transition Cases

## Case 1: SPY daily, late May to early June 2026

Pattern tags:
- `range-to-trend-transition`
- `trend-failure`

Context:
- SPY moved from orderly continuation into a high-pressure test of `760`
- the apparent upside resolution failed quickly

Why it matters:
- transitions are fragile
- a market can look like it has left a range, then fall right back into two-sided behavior

Operational takeaway:
- a breakout from balance should not be trusted until follow-through proves it

## Case 2: TSLA daily, 2026-05-26 to 2026-06-05

Pattern tags:
- `range-to-trend-transition`
- `downside-resolution`

Context:
- TSLA consolidated in the `433-442` area in late May
- downside break into early June carried to `391`

Correct interpretation:
- once the range resolved lower with real follow-through, the market stopped being neutral and started rewarding bearish continuation

Operational takeaway:
- ranges become trends only when one side stops failing

## Case 3: NVDA daily, 2026-05-26 to 2026-06-12

Pattern tags:
- `range-to-trend-transition`
- `failed-resolution`

Context:
- NVDA spent late May and early June trying to resolve from a relatively tight upper range
- the downside eventually dominated instead of a clean renewed acceleration

Why it matters:
- leadership stocks often get treated as if every pause is a bull flag

Operational takeaway:
- a former leader can transition from range to downside trend if bulls fail to create decisive follow-through quickly enough
