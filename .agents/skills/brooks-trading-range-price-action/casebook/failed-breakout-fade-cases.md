# Failed Breakout Fade Cases

## Case 1: AVGO daily, 2026-06-03 to 2026-06-05

Pattern tags:
- `failed-breakout`
- `range-fade`

Context:
- AVGO printed `495.00` high on `2026-06-03`
- the market treated the earnings-driven extension as unsustainable

Observed setup:
- apparent upside breakout
- immediate reversal and gap damage the next day

Why it looked attractive:
- strong thematic leadership
- new high breakout psychology

Why the fade was correct:
- breakout had no durable follow-through
- reversal was too strong to dismiss as ordinary profit-taking

Operational takeaway:
- when a breakout fails immediately after a climactic extension, fading or avoiding continuation is superior to hope

## Case 2: TSLA daily, 2026-05-11 to 2026-05-19

Pattern tags:
- `failed-breakout`
- `trend-to-range-downgrade`

Context:
- TSLA printed new highs near `445`
- within days it was trading near `404`

Why it mattered:
- strong momentum names often punish late breakout buyers hardest

Operational takeaway:
- breakout quality is not just about the breakout bar; it is about what the market does right after it

## Case 3: BTC-USD daily, 2026-05-22 to 2026-06-05

Pattern tags:
- `failed-breakout`
- `wedge-exhaustion`

Context:
- BTC repeatedly tried to hold elevated levels after the May high
- downside resolution became the dominant path

Operational takeaway:
- repeated upside attempts with weaker follow-through usually belong in the failed-breakout family, not in the healthy-consolidation family

## Case 4: SPY daily, 2026-06-02 to 2026-06-10

Pattern tags:
- `failed-breakout`
- `trend-to-range-downgrade`

Context:
- SPY printed `760.40` on `2026-06-02`
- within sessions it failed into the `725` area

Why it looked attractive:
- indices near highs often invite breakout trust

Why the fade or avoidance was right:
- the breakout never created stable one-sided continuation
- once the market returned deep into prior balance, the upside thesis was structurally revoked

Operational takeaway:
- failed breakout fades are not only for volatile single names; index products can produce them too when late continuation runs out of sponsorship
