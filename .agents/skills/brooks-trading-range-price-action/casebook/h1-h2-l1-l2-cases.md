# H1 H2 L1 L2 Cases

## Case 1: SPY 15m H2-style long, 2026-06-12

Pattern tags:
- `h2-continuation`

Context:
- after the early pullback sequence, the market needed more than one attempt to resume upward cleanly

Observed setup:
- first push higher lacked persistence
- second attempt from the `10:45-11:00 ET` sequence had better strength

Why it was useful:
- second entry gave clearer information than the first hesitant push

Operational takeaway:
- H2 logic is often cleaner than buying the first sign of life in a noisy recovery

## Case 2: AVGO daily L2-style continuation pressure, 2026-06-08 to 2026-06-12

Pattern tags:
- `l2-continuation`

Context:
- after the earnings collapse, AVGO kept trying to bounce
- each bounce struggled to reclaim the broken zone

Correct interpretation:
- the broader context still favored downside or repair, not immediate durable reversal

Operational takeaway:
- second-entry logic is strongest when aligned with the dominant structural side

## Case 3: BTC-USD hourly second-chance long versus daily damage, 2026-06-12 to 2026-06-13

Pattern tags:
- `h2-continuation`
- `multi-timeframe-conflict`

Context:
- hourly BTC-USD action on `2026-06-12` into `2026-06-13` showed repeated attempts to lift from the `63k` region
- some of those attempts looked like usable second-chance longs on the lower timeframe

Why it was tricky:
- hourly structure improved
- daily chart still reflected heavy prior damage from the early-June collapse

Operational takeaway:
- H2 logic can be valid intraday even when the higher timeframe argues for lower expectations
