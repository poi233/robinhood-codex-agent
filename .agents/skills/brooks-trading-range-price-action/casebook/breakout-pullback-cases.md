# Breakout Pullback Cases

## Case 1: SPY 15m, 2026-06-12 morning

Pattern tags:
- `breakout-pullback`
- `session-regime-shift`

Context:
- SPY had already stabilized after the `2026-06-10` washout
- on `2026-06-12`, the `10:45 ET` bar drove strongly from the `737-742` zone

Observed setup:
- strong bull signal bar
- immediate follow-through to `744.44` by `11:15 ET`

Why it looked attractive:
- the breakout came from a recognized prior breakout area
- follow-through arrived fast

Why it was valid:
- first pullback behavior stayed constructive long enough to pay continuation longs

Operational takeaway:
- in a recovery tape, the first strong breakout pullback can be the cleanest long of the session

## Case 2: AAPL daily, 2026-05-01 to 2026-05-05

Pattern tags:
- `breakout-pullback`
- `trend-resumption`

Context:
- AAPL separated upward on `2026-05-01`
- the next pullback held into `2026-05-04`

Observed setup:
- traders who missed the breakout got a calmer retest opportunity

Why it was valid:
- pullback stayed above the old balance zone
- the next leg resumed upward quickly

Operational takeaway:
- breakout pullbacks often offer better asymmetry than chasing the initial breakout bar

## Case 3: SPY daily, 2026-05-26 to 2026-06-02

Pattern tags:
- `breakout-pullback`
- `late-trend-example`

Context:
- SPY was still producing orderly upside behavior into the `758-760` zone

Observed setup:
- shallow pauses kept being bought
- continuation remained tradable before the later failure

Why it matters:
- breakout pullback logic can still work late in a move until the market actually loses follow-through

Operational takeaway:
- do not confuse "late in trend" with "already invalid"; trade quality changes only when behavior changes
