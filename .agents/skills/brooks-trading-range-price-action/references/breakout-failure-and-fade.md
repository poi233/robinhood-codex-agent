# Breakout Failure and Fade

## Core Idea

Most breakout attempts fail. A failed breakout is not random noise; it is a change in who dominated at the breakout point.

## Signs of Failure

- the breakout cannot get follow-through
- a reversal bar quickly erases the breakout
- the move returns to the breakout point
- overlap expands after the breakout

## Fade Logic

Fade is strongest when:

- breakout occurred at the edge of a larger range
- breakout lacked follow-through
- reversal comes as a second signal
- there is room back toward the opposite side of the range or a magnet

## Common Error

Fading a genuinely strong breakout just because it feels overextended.
