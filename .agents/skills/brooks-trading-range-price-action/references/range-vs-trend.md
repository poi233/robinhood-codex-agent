# Range vs Trend

## Default Stance

When uncertain, assume the market is in a trading range.

## Trend Behavior

A trend usually shows:

- directional bars with decent bodies
- shallow pullbacks
- follow-through after breakouts
- traders paying up in the trend direction

## Range Behavior

A range usually shows:

- overlap
- alternating bull and bear bars
- failed breakouts
- weak follow-through
- two-sided decision-making

## Practical Rule

If the current breakout is still inside a larger visible range on the left side of the chart, do not assume a new trend too early.

## Common Error

Calling a spike a trend when it is only a fast move from one side of the range to the other.
