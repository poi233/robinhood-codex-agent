# Common Misreads

## Misread 1: Spike Equals Trend

A fast move is treated as a new trend even though it remains inside a larger range.

## Misread 2: First Signal Worship

The first reversal bar is treated as enough proof even though Brooks logic often favors the second signal.

## Misread 3: Ignoring Overlap

The trader mentally stays in trend mode after price action has already degraded into range behavior.

## Misread 4: Shape Without Context

Wedge, double top, or breakout pullback is traded without checking location and larger structure.

## Misread 5: No Trade Equation

Setup looks good, but reward is too small for the risk.
