# H1, H2, L1, L2

## Purpose

These are bar-counting entries that organize pullback structure.

## High 1 and High 2

In a bull context:

- High 1 is the first attempt to end a pullback
- High 2 is the second attempt and is often more reliable

## Low 1 and Low 2

In a bear context:

- Low 1 is the first attempt to end a rally
- Low 2 is the second attempt and is often more reliable

## Why Second Entries Matter

The first attempt often fails. The second attempt packages:

- a failed first reversal
- clearer context
- better information about who still controls the market

## High 3 and Low 3

A third push often behaves like a wedge. It can still work, but exhaustion risk is higher.

## Common Error

Taking every H1 or L1 mechanically without context, especially inside trading ranges.
