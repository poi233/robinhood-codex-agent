# Interval Nesting

## Purpose

Interval nesting uses a lower level to improve entry timing inside a higher-level thesis.

## Correct Order

1. Build the thesis on the execution level.
2. Confirm the higher-level signal is valid.
3. Drop to the lower level only to reduce slippage or tighten invalidation.

## What It Is Not

It is not permission to invent a trade from lower-level noise when the higher level has no valid setup.

## Good Uses

- locating a cleaner type 2 trigger inside a higher-level reversal
- finding a better third-buy timing after a higher-level pullback holds
- tightening entry in fast markets without changing the thesis

## Bad Uses

- taking a lower-level long directly into a higher-level center ceiling
- using lower-level momentum to ignore higher-level structural damage
- changing a failed higher-level read into a new lower-level thesis just to avoid admitting the failure

## Output Discipline

Always state:

- higher-level thesis
- lower-level trigger
- whether invalidation belongs to the higher or lower level
