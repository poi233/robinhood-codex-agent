# Fractals, Strokes, Segments

## Purpose

Chan analysis depends on decomposition. If the decomposition is unstable, every later conclusion becomes unstable.

## Fractals

Fractals mark local turning points. Their job is not to predict; their job is to mark candidate structural pivots.

Use them to ask:

- has this local move completed enough to define a turn?
- is the chart forming a sequence of higher pivots or lower pivots?

## Strokes

A stroke connects one meaningful turning area to the next. A usable stroke should represent a real directional push, not random bar noise.

Good stroke behavior:

- directional clarity
- visible separation from the prior move
- supports later comparison of force

Bad stroke behavior:

- forced counting through overlap
- counting every tiny fluctuation
- changing the count every few bars

## Segments

Segments are built from strokes and matter because trends and centers are judged at this scale.

Use segments to decide:

- whether the move is building trend continuation
- whether a center is forming
- whether a buy or sell point is early, retest-based, or continuation-based

## Stability Rule

Do not keep re-drawing structure because the latest bar is emotionally important. Recount only when the grammar actually changes.

## Working Discipline

For any live chart:

1. Mark candidate pivots.
2. Connect them into strokes.
3. Ask whether the current sequence yields a segment or only noise.
4. Use segments, not raw candles, to judge centers and trend state.

## Common Errors

- counting too finely in choppy zones
- defining strokes with inconsistent standards
- using unresolved pivots as if they were confirmed structure
