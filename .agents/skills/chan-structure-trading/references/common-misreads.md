# Common Misreads

## Misread 1: Fake Third Buy

Price seems to leave the center, but the pullback later re-enters it. This is center extension, not a valid third buy.

## Misread 2: Lower-Level Reversal Against Higher-Level Damage

A sharp lower-level bounce is mistaken for a meaningful reversal even though the higher-level structure is still broken.

## Misread 3: Divergence Without Structural Completion

Momentum weakens, but the leg is not clearly complete and the old trend has not actually failed.

## Misread 4: Constant Recounting

The analyst changes the decomposition every few bars. This usually means the rules are being bent to fit emotion.

## Misread 5: Level Drift

Entry, stop, and thesis migrate across timeframes. This produces inconsistent decisions.

## Prevention Checklist

- name the execution level
- locate the last center
- identify whether the setup is reversal or continuation
- define invalidation on the same level
- confirm the signal still exists after the latest bar
