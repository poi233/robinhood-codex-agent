# Centers and Trends

## Center First

If you do not know where the center is, you do not know where a continuation or reversal should be judged.

## What a Center Does

A center is the overlap zone that organizes price conflict. It tells you:

- where balance is happening
- where continuation must prove itself
- where re-entry invalidates a continuation read

## Trend vs Center Extension

Two common live states:

- trend continuation: price leaves a center and keeps building away from it
- center extension: price appears to leave, but later expands the same balance area instead

The second case traps traders who mistake temporary movement for true continuation.

## Judgment Questions

Ask:

1. Is price still structurally tied to the last center?
2. Did the latest pullback re-enter the center?
3. Did the next move build a new leg or only enlarge the old overlap?

## Trend Reading

A trend is stronger when:

- successive segments separate cleanly
- pullbacks respect the center boundary
- continuation signals do not collapse back into prior balance

A trend is weaker when:

- departures from the center repeatedly fail
- overlap grows instead of resolves
- force decreases as price makes late extremes

## Common Errors

- calling every breakout from overlap a new trend
- ignoring that repeated re-entry often means center extension
- trying to identify buy or sell points before locating the active center
