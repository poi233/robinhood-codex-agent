# Divergence and Force

## Divergence Is a Comparison

Divergence matters only when compared against a prior meaningful leg. It is not "indicator looks weaker." It is "the new push achieves less force than the old push in the same structural role."

## What to Compare

Compare:

- length and clarity of the move
- ability to separate from balance
- reaction after the move
- supporting momentum measures if used

MACD can help, but it cannot define structure on its own.

## Strong vs Weak Force

Stronger force:

- clean directional separation
- shallow corrective interruption
- quick follow-through

Weaker force:

- overlapping continuation
- repeated hesitation near the extreme
- strong reaction against the move

## Use in Trading

- in type 1 setups, divergence is the core warning of exhaustion
- in continuation setups, poor force warns that a "breakout" may only become center extension

## Common Errors

- using indicator divergence without a complete structural comparison
- calling divergence too early before a leg is actually finished
- ignoring price behavior because an indicator still looks strong
