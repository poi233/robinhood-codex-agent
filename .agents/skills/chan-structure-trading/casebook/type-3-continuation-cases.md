# Type 3 Continuation Cases

## Case 1: AAPL daily, 2026-04-17 to 2026-05-22

Pattern tags:
- `type3-continuation`
- `center-breakout`

Context:
- late-April overlap sat roughly in the `268-273` zone
- AAPL broke upward and then pulled back without materially re-entering that old balance

Observed setup:
- `2026-05-01` opened the upside separation
- `2026-05-04` low near `274.86` held above the old center zone

Why it looked attractive:
- the market had already declared direction before the pullback
- the pullback behaved like verification, not structural damage

Why it was valid:
- old balance did not reabsorb price
- the next leg carried toward `308-312`

Correct interpretation:
- clean type 3 continuation buy

Operational takeaway:
- the best continuation entries often come from the first disciplined hold after separation, not from chasing the breakout bar

## Case 2: SPY daily, 2026-05-26 to 2026-06-02

Pattern tags:
- `type3-continuation`
- `late-trend-example`

Context:
- SPY climbed from `750.59` on `2026-05-26` to `759.57` on `2026-06-02`
- the move respected the prior higher-level advance for several sessions

Observed setup:
- each shallow pause still held as continuation for a time

Why it looked attractive:
- upside structure remained intact until the sharp failure started

Why it was valid at the time:
- separation from prior balance held long enough to justify continuation

Correct interpretation:
- this is a useful reminder that valid type 3 continuation can still happen late in a move; late does not mean automatically false

Operational takeaway:
- a continuation read should be judged by structure, not by trader fatigue; only after re-entry or real damage does the thesis fail

## Case 3: BTC-USD daily, 2026-05-20 to 2026-05-25

Pattern tags:
- `type3-continuation`
- `late-trend-example`

Context:
- BTC bounced from the `76k` area and held together into `2026-05-25`
- continuation was still possible before the later breakdown phase accelerated

Why it matters:
- not every late trend attempt is false from the start
- the market can still produce valid continuation structures before finally failing

Correct interpretation:
- this stretch is useful as a reminder that continuation and exhaustion can coexist in close sequence

Operational takeaway:
- do not call a setup false just because it happens late; call it false only when structure actually fails
