# Type 1 Reversal Cases

## Case 1: AVGO daily, 2026-06-02 to 2026-06-05

Pattern tags:
- `type1-reversal`
- `false-breakout`

Context:
- AVGO pushed from `446.77` on `2026-05-29` to `495.00` high on `2026-06-03`.
- The move accelerated into earnings and looked like a textbook late-stage momentum surge.

Observed setup:
- price made a fresh extreme
- emotional participation increased
- the first hard response was not a mild pullback but a structural break

Why it looked attractive:
- new highs in a strong AI narrative stock
- no obvious resistance overhead

Why it was valid:
- the push into `495` was climactic, not orderly
- the first reversal response was violent enough to destroy the continuation grammar
- `2026-06-04` opened the post-earnings break toward `418.91`, confirming that the prior high was not durable trend separation

Correct interpretation:
- this was a type 1 sell-style reversal environment: late-force exhaustion followed by a decisive rejection

Operational takeaway:
- when a mature trend makes a dramatic final extension, the first reversal leg matters more than the story

## Case 2: BTC-USD daily, 2026-05-10 to 2026-06-05

Pattern tags:
- `type1-reversal`
- `wedge-exhaustion`

Context:
- BTC-USD peaked at `82,138.93` on `2026-05-10`
- later highs and rebounds failed to re-establish the same expansion quality

Observed setup:
- the market stayed near highs for a while, but upside force degraded
- repeated recovery attempts led to poorer separation

Why it looked attractive:
- price was still near the upper part of the prior range
- traders could argue the trend was simply consolidating

Why it was valid:
- the early-June break from `73k` to `60k` showed that the prior highs were distribution, not healthy rest
- the final upswings had weaker continuation quality than the earlier thrust

Correct interpretation:
- type 1 reversal was not a single candle; it was a late-stage exhaustion zone confirmed by structural failure

Operational takeaway:
- type 1 setups do not need perfect indicator symmetry; they need a clearly weakening late trend plus decisive loss of structure

## Case 3: TSLA daily, 2026-05-11 to 2026-05-19

Pattern tags:
- `type1-reversal`
- `climactic-extension`

Context:
- TSLA surged to `445.00` on `2026-05-11` and `445.27` on `2026-05-13`
- only a few sessions later it was trading near `404.11`

Observed setup:
- strong upside bars invited breakout chasing
- reversal arrived quickly after the surge

Why it looked attractive:
- headline momentum still favored continuation

Why it was valid:
- the move could not convert the new highs into stable continuation
- price rapidly lost altitude and failed to hold the breakout zone

Correct interpretation:
- this is a type 1 reversal template where the first down leg invalidates the emotional breakout thesis

Operational takeaway:
- in volatile momentum names, late upside strength can be the precondition for reversal rather than continuation

## Case 4: SPY daily, 2026-06-01 to 2026-06-10

Pattern tags:
- `type1-reversal`
- `late-trend-failure`

Context:
- SPY advanced to `760.40` on `2026-06-02`
- less than a week later it traded down to `725.43` on `2026-06-10`

Observed setup:
- the market was still printing near highs, but the continuation quality was deteriorating
- the downside response after the high was too deep to treat as normal pause behavior

Why it looked attractive:
- broad indices often get the benefit of the doubt in strong trends
- many traders expected the `758-760` area to be just another shallow breakout zone

Why it was valid:
- the first reversal leg was not a mild reset; it destroyed the prior short-term continuation grammar
- once price broke into the `735-725` area, the burden shifted from bulls to anyone still insisting the uptrend was intact

Correct interpretation:
- this is a broad-market type 1 reversal template: late highs, then a hard first breakdown that forces reassessment

Operational takeaway:
- index charts can hide exhaustion better than single-name charts; the first real break often matters more than the visual smoothness before it
