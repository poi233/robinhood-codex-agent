# Type 2 Retest Cases

## Case 1: AAPL daily sell-side retest, 2026-06-08 to 2026-06-12

Pattern tags:
- `type2-retest`
- `repair-rally`

Context:
- AAPL dropped from `317.40` intraday high on `2026-06-08` to `290.55` close on `2026-06-09`
- the first down leg clearly damaged the prior short-term advance

Observed setup:
- `2026-06-11` and `2026-06-12` bounced the stock back toward `295-297`
- the rebound looked constructive to traders hunting a fast recovery

Why it looked attractive:
- the bounce was sharp enough to feel like a bottom
- the company is a high-quality name, making traders more willing to trust the first rebound

Why it was only a retest:
- the bounce did not restore the broken short-term structure
- price stayed well below the prior `315-317` zone

Correct interpretation:
- this is closer to a type 2 sell-side retest lesson than a fresh long thesis: after the first damage, the market tested repair quality and failed to prove full reversal

Operational takeaway:
- a type 2 retest is cleaner than pure type 1, but it still needs proof that the old break has actually been absorbed

## Case 2: SPY daily buy-side retest candidate, 2026-06-10 to 2026-06-12

Pattern tags:
- `type2-retest`
- `candidate-not-confirmed`

Context:
- SPY washed out to `725.43` on `2026-06-10` after reaching `760.40` only days earlier
- `2026-06-11` and `2026-06-12` produced a quick rebound to `741.75`

Observed setup:
- the first panic leg finished
- the rebound showed the market was willing to defend the washout zone

Why it looked attractive:
- broad index recovery after panic often produces cleaner second-chance entries

Why it remained only a candidate:
- rebound strength was real but still far below the `758-760` damage zone
- a full reversal case still needed a stronger retest and reclaim

Correct interpretation:
- useful type 2 buy candidate, but not yet a fully proven trend resumption

Operational takeaway:
- type 2 logic can justify attention before full recovery, but "candidate" and "confirmed" are not the same trade quality

## Case 3: NVDA daily, 2026-06-05 to 2026-06-12

Pattern tags:
- `type2-retest`
- `candidate-not-confirmed`

Context:
- NVDA broke down from `224.36` on `2026-06-01` to `200.42` on `2026-06-10`
- rebounds on `2026-06-11` and `2026-06-12` lifted price only back to `205.19`

Observed setup:
- the first panic leg had already happened
- traders were now testing whether the bounce could become a real second-chance long

Why it looked attractive:
- strong leadership stocks often produce good type 2 reversals after panic flushes

Why it remained incomplete:
- the bounce stayed too close to the damaged zone
- price had not repaired enough of the prior decline to prove a stronger regime change

Correct interpretation:
- useful type 2 watchlist case, but not yet a strong reversal confirmation

Operational takeaway:
- with former leaders, traders often confuse "not going down anymore" with "already repaired"
