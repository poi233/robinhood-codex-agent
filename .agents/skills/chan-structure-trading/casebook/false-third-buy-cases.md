# False Third Buy Cases

## Case 1: BTC-USD daily bounce, 2026-06-07 to 2026-06-12

Pattern tags:
- `false-third-buy`
- `repair-rally`

Context:
- BTC-USD had already collapsed from the low `80k` area to around `60k`
- `2026-06-07` bounced to `63,239`, and later sessions kept trying to hold the rebound

Observed setup:
- the rebound was strong enough to tempt continuation buyers

Why it looked attractive:
- violent down moves often produce sharp reflex rallies
- traders often mistake that for a fresh continuation base

Why it was false:
- the bounce never repaired the larger structural damage
- price remained deep inside the broken zone created by the early-June collapse

Correct interpretation:
- repair rally, not clean third buy

Operational takeaway:
- after catastrophic structure damage, do not call a bounce a third buy unless a new higher-level base clearly exists

## Case 2: SPY daily repair, 2026-06-11 to 2026-06-12

Pattern tags:
- `false-third-buy`
- `multi-timeframe-conflict`

Context:
- SPY rebounded from `725.43` to `741.75`
- intraday action improved, but daily damage from the `760` zone remained unresolved

Observed setup:
- price action looked much better than the panic low

Why it looked attractive:
- market participants like to call sharp rebounds in index products "the new low"

Why it was false or premature:
- the larger damaged zone had not been reclaimed
- the rebound was still repair inside a compromised structure

Correct interpretation:
- not a reliable daily third buy yet

Operational takeaway:
- lower-level recovery can coexist with higher-level non-confirmation

## Case 3: NVDA daily bounce, 2026-06-10 to 2026-06-12

Pattern tags:
- `false-third-buy`
- `below-moving-average`

Context:
- NVDA fell from `224.36` on `2026-06-01` to `200.42` on `2026-06-10`
- bounce to `205.19` on `2026-06-12` looked stabilizing

Why it looked attractive:
- large-cap leadership names often attract reflex dip buyers

Why it was false or too early:
- the bounce remained near or below the damaged short-term structure
- no clear new continuation base had formed

Operational takeaway:
- a recognizable name and a quick bounce are not enough to declare third-buy continuation

## Case 4: AVGO daily bounce, 2026-06-08 to 2026-06-12

Pattern tags:
- `false-third-buy`
- `earnings-gap-damage`

Context:
- AVGO collapsed from `479.23` on `2026-06-03` to `372.10` by `2026-06-10`
- bounce to `385.57` and `382.07` looked like stabilization

Why it looked attractive:
- sharp selloffs often create tempting "washed-out" continuation narratives

Why it was false or too early:
- the post-earnings gap damage had not been repaired
- price remained far inside the broken zone created by `418-495`

Correct interpretation:
- this was a repair bounce after structural destruction, not a reliable third-buy continuation

Operational takeaway:
- gap damage from catalyst events needs more proof than ordinary pullbacks before continuation language becomes valid
