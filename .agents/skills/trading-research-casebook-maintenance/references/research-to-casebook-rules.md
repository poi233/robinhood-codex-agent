# Research to Casebook Rules

## Core Principle

The casebook is a judgment library, not an archive of everything researched.

## Add A Case When

- the pattern is reusable
- the mistake is likely to recur
- the case changes how future setups should be interpreted
- the case teaches boundary discipline between two similar-looking situations

## Do Not Add A Case When

- the note only repeats what already exists
- the setup is too vague to generalize
- the lesson depends on a one-off headline with no durable pattern

## Preferred Case Shape

Good cases answer:

- what happened
- why a smart analyst might misread it
- what the correct read was
- what to do differently next time

## Update Discipline

- append to the smallest correct pattern file
- avoid duplicating the same lesson across many files unless the cross-skill link is truly useful
- keep the language concrete and decision-oriented
